## Key Findings

### 1. Match Quality Is High Across the Fleet

All three warehouses show strong match quality with clean data integrity. The embedding pipeline correctly processed all records, with no unexplained gaps or silent failures. Every Exact match is rule-proven; every fuzzy match has a traceable AI confidence score.

### 2. Two Distinct Warehouse Profiles Emerged

**Warehouse 115** behaves differently from 827 and 947:

- **Warehouse 115** has a higher mean score (90.5) and a manageable review queue (54%). Nearly half of all matches auto-close at high confidence.
- **Warehouses 827 and 947** cluster tightly just below the 90-point threshold (means of 87.9 and 88.1), resulting in ~80% of matches landing in the review queue.

This divergence means one warehouse's operational experience does not directly predict another's.

### 3. The 90-Point Threshold Is the Critical Decision Boundary

Across all warehouses, the score distribution peaks in the 88-90 range. This means the 90-point boundary between "auto-close as high-confidence" and "send to review" is the single most operationally impactful threshold:

- **Warehouse 115**: 3,185 rows sit within 2 points of the 90 cutoff
- **Warehouses 827/947**: The peak bin is [89, 90) -- one point below auto-close

Small score changes at this boundary move hundreds of matches between automated closure and manual review.

## Recommendation

**Calibrate thresholds with a human-labeled validation set.**

The scoring rules (floor at 70, bands at 70/85/90, weighted formula) are fixed and working as designed. No rule changes are needed. However, the operational impact of these fixed bands varies by warehouse -- 54% review queue in WH 115 vs. 80%+ in WH 827/947.

A targeted validation exercise (human reviewers labeling a sample of matches in the 85-92 range) will determine whether the current thresholds deliver the right precision/recall tradeoff per warehouse, and whether the review workload can be safely reduced.

### Suggested Next Steps

1. **Label a validation sample** -- 200-300 matches per warehouse from the 85-92 score range, reviewed by business users for true/false match
2. **Measure precision at each band** -- confirm that "Matching High" (90+) truly represents high-quality matches
3. **Evaluate per-warehouse calibration** -- determine if WH 827/947 need adjusted operational thresholds vs. WH 115
4. **Scale to additional warehouses** -- the analysis pipeline is automated and ready to run on any new warehouse with completed match data
